Token Optimization

Executive Summary

For a logistics provider processing thousands of invoices, optimized prompts cut token usage by 40%. The resulting API savings lowered monthly spend while keeping accuracy high.

About the Client

Our client manages warehousing and last-mile delivery for hundreds of online retailers. With operations spread across multiple time zones, their internal support chatbot sees constant activity from drivers and dispatchers looking for shipment updates.

The In-Depth Challenge

Verbose prompts were inflating API costs and slowing response times. The bot often included unnecessary system messages that consumed tokens without adding value. Over the course of a month, usage exceeded budget by 60% and forced the company to throttle interactions, frustrating the fleet.

A Technical Deep Dive

We refined prompts and implemented caching so only essential tokens were sent to the model. First, we audited chat logs to identify common intents and trimmed boilerplate text. We introduced embeddings-based similarity search to retrieve previous responses, avoiding repeated calls to the API. Additionally, a custom middleware layer compressed large location payloads into short tokens. The diagram below shows how the caching layer routes requests.

Quantifiable Results and ROI

Token usage fell by 40%, delivering significant monthly savings. Response times improved by 50%, leading to faster dispatch decisions and happier drivers. Within four months, the project paid for itself.
